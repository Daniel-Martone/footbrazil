Brazil's national team

The Brazil's national team, founded in 1915 is with no doubt one of the biggest Brazilian symbol, as known as "Canarinho", "Verde-Amarela", "Brasilzão", among others.

It is the most victorious national team in the history, with 5 world cups, being the first and only to reach this mark.

From left, Pelé, Garrincha, Fenômeno and Ronaldinho

Sweden, 1958

In 1958 the Brazil's golden age began with its first world title.

In june 29th, with an amazing team, having names like Pelé, Vavá and Garrincha Brazil won against Sweden, the country hosting 58s world cup in a 5-2 match. Highlight for Pelé, who scored 2 goals in the finals when he was only 17 years old, being the youngest to win a world cup to date

Chile, 1962

It's june 10th 1962, Brazil was playing against England, the beggining of the game was tough for Brazil, with the English team opening the score.

Amarildo tied the game, Zito turned the game and Vavá scored the 3rd goal, being the only human to ever score on two finals in-a-row.

Mexico, 1970

What most say its the greatest Brazilian title of all time, the 70s world cup was hosted in Mexico.

After a disaster in the 66s world cup, Brazil came to achieve its 3rd world cup in a 12 years time span, being the first national team to have 3 world titles, as, winning the Jules Rimet, that was planned to be given to the first three-time champion team. After some years, the Jules Rimet was stolen and melt.

An easy game for Brazil against Italy, by the time also two-times world champion, garanteed the title for brazil in a 4-1 match.
